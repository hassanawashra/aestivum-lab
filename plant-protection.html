Crop Infection vs. Crop Infestation

Infection and infestation are two different forms of parasitic attacks on the host but differ by the type of the attacker.

In case of attacks by microorganisms (fungi, bacteria, yeast, viruses,, etc.), this attack will be referred as infection, in which the parasitic organism will be living inside the host or on its outer tissues. However, infestation refers to the parasitic action of parasites attacking the hosts to obtain nutrients or a specific preferred environment. This includes invasions by insects, weeds, rodents, birds, animals, and any macro organism that could cause a threat for the crops.

Understanding the differences and management strategies for these problems is crucial for effective plant protection
