Crop Infection vs. Crop Infestation

Infection and infestation are two different forms of parasitic attacks on the crop but differ by the type of the parasite.

In case of attacks by microorganisms (fungi, bacteria, yeast, viruses, etc.), it will be referred as infection, in which the parasitic organism will be living inside the host tissues/organs or on its outer tissues. However, infestation refers to the parasitic action of parasites attacking the hosts to obtain nutrients or a specific preferred environment. This includes invasions by insects, weeds, rodents, birds, animals, and any macro organism that could cause a threat for the crop health or reduce its yeild.

Understanding the differences and management strategies for these parasitic regimes is crucial for effective plant protection
